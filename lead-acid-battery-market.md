<p><p>The lead-acid battery market comprises two main types: VRLA (Valve-Regulated Lead-Acid) batteries and flooded batteries. VRLA batteries are sealed and maintenance-free, making them suitable for applications requiring reliable backup power, such as renewable energy systems and uninterruptible power supplies. In contrast, flooded batteries have liquid electrolyte and require regular maintenance, typically used in applications like automotive and industrial settings. Both types serve critical roles in energy storage but differ in maintenance needs and design features.</p></p>
