<p><ul><li>Pars Plana Vitrectomy</li><li>Anterior Vitrectomy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pediatric vitrectomy market is primarily categorized into two types: Pars Plana Vitrectomy (PPV) and Anterior Vitrectomy. PPV involves accessing the vitreous cavity through the pars plana region, typically performed for conditions like retinal detachment and vitreous hemorrhage in children. It allows for extensive manipulation within the eye. On the other hand, Anterior Vitrectomy focuses on the removal of vitreous gel in the anterior segment, often used in trauma cases or cataract surgeries to prevent complications. Both techniques are crucial for managing pediatric retinal disorders.</p></p>
