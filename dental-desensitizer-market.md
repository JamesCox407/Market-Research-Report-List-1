<p><p>The dental desensitizer market is categorized into fluoride-containing and fluoride-free products. Fluoride-containing dental desensitizers are designed to strengthen tooth enamel, reduce sensitivity by blocking nerve impulses, and provide added protection against decay. In contrast, fluoride-free dental desensitizers offer alternative ingredients, such as potassium nitrate or calcium phosphates, targeting sensitivity without fluoride. Both types aim to alleviate discomfort associated with dental procedures and everyday activities, catering to diverse consumer preferences and dental health needs.</p></p>
